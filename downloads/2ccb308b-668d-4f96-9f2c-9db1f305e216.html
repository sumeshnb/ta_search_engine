Former CSE Faculty Member Eric Byrne Dies While on Himalayan Trek

Tuesday, May 10, 2005

Eric Byrne, a former faculty member in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at UTA, died May 9 after being stricken with an illness while trekking in the Himalayas. He was 44.

Dr. Byrne of Ocean, N.J., died from complications of high-altitude sickness. He was on a trekking expedition in Bhutan at 13,000 feet when he became ill May 5.

Members of his trekking party and Bhutanese soldiers carried him for 18 hours by litter to a base camp, where he was transported by helicopter May 7 to a Bhutanese hospital. Dr. Byrne appeared to be recovering and was awaiting transport to a hospital in Bangkok when he suddenly died.

He was buried May 23 in Omaha, Neb.

Dr. Byrne served on the faculty of CSE from 1993 to 1997. "Dr. Byrne was a highly esteemed member of the CSE faculty and very much well liked by his colleagues and students," said Behrooz Shirazi, chair of CSE. "We are greatly saddened by Dr. Byrne's sudden, untimely death and extend to his family and friends our deepest condolences."

Dr. Byrne, who was working as a consultant at the time of his death, earned his master's and doctoral degree in computer science from Kansas State University and his bachelor's degree from the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. He also earned an MBA from Wharton Business School at the University of Pennsylvania.

An affiliate of the Wharton Business School, Wharton Leadership Ventures, was the sponsor of the Himalayan trek Dr. Byrne was on when he became ill. It was the fourth Himalayan trek he had made through the program.

Dr. Byrne is survived by his parents, Joseph C. and Eleanor J. Byrne of Omaha; a sister, Carey Byrne and her husband, Ernest Newmann, of Brookfield, Ill.; a nephew, Andrew Paul Newmann; and his companion, Ashley Griffin of Panama City, Fla.

Memorials can be made to the Andrew and Sarah Newmann Education Fund.
