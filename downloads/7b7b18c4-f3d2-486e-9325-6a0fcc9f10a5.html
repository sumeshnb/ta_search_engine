PerCom Is Leading International Conference on Pervasive Computing

Saturday, October 15, 2005

Computer scientists worldwide seeking current state-of-the-art research on pervasive computing and communications are turning to PerCom, the leading international conference on pervasive computing, which is sponsored by the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at UTA and IEEE.

 CSE Professor Mohan Kumar, left, general chair of PerCom 2005, and Behrooz Shirazi, chair of CSE and PerCom Steering Committee Chair, in Hawaii. 

More PerCom photos

The conference, scheduled for Italy in 2006, has gained international prominence in a short time. PerCom was founded at UTA by Behrooz Shirazi, CSE department chair, and CSE professors Sajal Das and Mohan Kumar in 2003. By 2005, the conference had established itself as the preeminent conference in pervasive computing and communications, drawing more than 200 leading researchers from academia and industry to Hawaii in March for the third annual conference. Dr. Kumar served as general chair of the 2005 conference and serves on the conference's steering committee along with Dr. Das. Dr. Shirazi serves as chair of the PerCom steering committee

PerCom 2005 attracted more than 230 research papers, with 31 full papers selected for presentation. Vipul Gupta and his associates at Sun Microsystems won Best Paper Award for their presentation "Sizzle: A Standards-based End-to-End Security Architecture for the Embedded Internet."

The conference program also included the presentation of eight short papers, seven workshops and two panel discussions. Keynote speeches were presented by Roy Campbell of the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign and Srikanta Kumar of DARPA. Product demonstrations included one on smart home technology from Michael Youngblood of UTA and one from IBM Research presented by Mandayam Raghunath on PC virtualization using portable devices.

PerCom attendees praised the high quality of the conference and the important technical value it delivered. The conference, held on the beautiful island of Kauai, helped ensure those attending had fun by hosting a large luau with dancers and live music, prompting some computer scientists to enthusiastically join in the merriment by dancing the hula with native entertainers.

"PerCom 2005 was an overwhelming success and has become the most sought after annual event for researchers in pervasive computing," said Dr. Mohan Kumar, who served as general chair of the 2005 conference and serves on the conference's steering committee along with Dr. Das. Dr. Shirazi serves as chair of the PerCom steering committee

Program chair for the conference was Klara Nahrstedt of the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

Industry sponsors for the conference were Nokia, IBM and Sun Microsystems.

PerCom will be held in Pisa, Italy in March 2006. The general chair for the 2006 conference is Enrico Gregori of the Istituto di Informatica e Telematica in Italy.

PerCom was founded under CSE's Top 25 Initiative, which is designed to help elevate the department to among the top CSE departments in the nation. The conference has helped raised awareness internationally about computer science and engineering at UTA, which has significant research in pervasive computing under way.
